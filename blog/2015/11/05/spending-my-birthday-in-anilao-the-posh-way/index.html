Spending My Birthday in Anilao - the Posh Way

Nov 5th, 2015 6:03 am

To celebrate my birthday last week me and Gen decided (in last minute) to get ourselves to Anilao. A popular macro diving site located only “three hours” away from Manila. Three hours Filipino time that is. You probably can make it in three hours if you decide to go with a private transport, however if you go with the local transport including a bus ride, a jeepney ride and finally a tricycle ride, you’re probably looking at 5 hours minimum.

There are several bus companies that does daily routes down to Batangas City, we chose the one called Jam Liner and paid roughly 125 php per person. When you arrive at the bus station in Batangas City you simply switch to one of the jeepneys going to Mabini (another 35 php per person). Be sure to tell the driver that you are going to Anilao so he can drop you off at the “the crossing” where you would continue the last part with a tricycle for about 120 php.

Even though I’m fully capable of carrying my own bag, this little kid insisted on helping me carry my bag from the bus to the jeepney. It was only about fifty meters and I told him several times that I would carry my own bag. Around half way he gave up, and I continued to the jeepney. Later when I was joking around with the jeepney-drivers the kid snuck up on me and lifted my bag into the jeepney. I laughed for my self and gave the boy some money. I would have loved to give him credit for his quick-witted mind but he disappeared. I like to think that this boy is going places if anybody just gave him the chance. Unfortunately the world is tough and far from everyone gets the chance they deserve.

We arrived quite late to this resort called Halo Anilao Dive Resort located far from the busy streets life of Anilao. The resort itself isn’t anything special, I mean the rooms were nice and the food was good, but the place it self was kind of “meh”. It kinda threw me off a bit, and we only stayed there one night. The next day we decided to go all in and chose a place called La Chevrerie Resort & Spa (after all, my sister told me to “spoil myself” since I’m always so stingy, I however consider myself as an economic person, I’ll leave it up for discussion though).

La Chevrerie Resort & Spa is a fantastic resort in every aspect, the rooms were amazing, the food was gorgeous, the view was stunning and the staff was extremely helpful and friendly. The only thing I could say something about is the price, but I guess you get what you pay for. And there are far more expensive resorts out there, but yes, La Chevrerie Resort & Spa did leave some sort of whole in my wallet ;)

The plan was to stay in the area for a couple of days, but we cancelled the holiday since Gen’s cat unfortunately passed away. Her name was Ate Guy (pronounced “ah-teh-guy”) and we brought her to the veterinarian two days earlier since she hadn’t been eating for a while and was very weak. The veterinarian did what they could and gave her antibiotics, but after two days Ate Guy sadly passed away. Gen rescued Ate Guy from the streets about seven years ago, during these years Ate Guy has had the best possible life. I’m sure Ate Guy has her place in cat heaven by now.
