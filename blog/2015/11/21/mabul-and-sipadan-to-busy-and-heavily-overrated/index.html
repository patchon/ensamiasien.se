Mabul & Sipadan - to Busy and Heavily Overpriced

Nov 21st, 2015 1:06 am

I decided to leave Manila and head over to Borneo for a second time. This time I really wanted to try out Sipadan and the diving around Mabul. Sipadan is probably one of the most famous dive destinations in Asia and its dive site Baracuda Point is well known amongst divers. Needless to say I had high expectations about Sipadan.

The thing that threw me off a little bit the last time I was on Borneo was the recent cases of kidnapping on Mabul and the recommendation “not to go there” from the Swedish Embassy. As rescent as last year one police officer killed got killed and another kidnapped so I think it’s only natural to have second thoughts about going there. After reading up about it though I felt pretty safe going there.

I headed off to Kota Kinabalu and spent the day there with some good friends. Later that night I flew off to Sandakan and spent the night at the airport. Now, sleeping at the airport is not as comfy as it sounds, it’s actually rather the opposite. I did however managed to sleep a few hours on a “steel bench” with my laundry bag as a pillow and catch my early morning flight to Tawau. My good friend Fizzy had ordered a pickup for me at the airport so the transfer from Tawau to Semporna was a breeze. I waited a few hours in Semporna and then finally we headed off to Mabul

Directly when we arrived at Mabul I got the feeling that this wasn’t the place for me. It’s extremely crowded and everything is very “robotic”, there is no “personal touch” to the place. It’s just way to busy for me, and you are “just one in the crowd”. I tend to like the abandoned places with as few people as possible. Fizzy had booked a few nights for me at a local homestay called “Panglima” which I enjoyed very much. Almost every day we had lunch at a local place that served some excellent kway teow. I had so many kway teow there that in the end I didn’t even had to order, I just looked at the waiter, smiled and nodded. A few days later we found a local börger place who served some excellent “double deluxe beef börgers” - we were living the high life.

The diving around Mabul didn’t really impress me. Again, it’s very crowded, the water is “greenish” and the visibility was far from great. With that being said though, I saw some cool stuff so I definitely not regret going there. But one, maximum two days of diving was enough for me.

Now, Sipadan is a different story. Sipadan is far from crowded, the island is clean and the visibility in the water is generally good (it wasn’t amazing during my four dives, but around 20m). The diving itself was alright, but I definitely wouldn’t rate it as one of my favourite dive sites. We saw quite a few white tip sharks, a huge school of jacks (this was actually really cool, a huge wall of jacks, literally thousands of thousands), a small school of barracudas (unfortunately we never saw the huge school of barracudas that barracuda point is so famous for), turtles, giant trevallys, school of bumphead parrotfish, and some beautiful scenery (if you’re lucky you’ll even get to see the hammerheads as well (I wasn’t though)).

So, why isn’t this my favourite dive site when it seems to be everyone else’s ? I’ve spoken to dive masters who literally has done over thousand dives at this place and just loves it. And this is exactly my point. A thousand dives is of course extreme, but since this is nature we are talking about (the visibility, the amount of fishes, the types of fishes etc.) I would need at least a few days of fun diving to be able to rate it “my favourite dive site”. Maybe I had a bad day at Sipadan, but I’ll never know for sure since I basically “can’t go there again”. Of course I can visit Sipadan, it’s not physically impossible, but the prices are just ridiculous. You’re looking at between 800-1000 ringit for one day diving around Sipadan, and that’s only for the day at Sipadan, most operators wont take you to Sipadan unless you do a couple of days fun diving with them around Mabul/Kapalai. The execution here is just terrible, but I don’t blame anyone, nor do I have a suggestion on how to do it better, it’s just a tough situation when the whole world wants to come together and dive and at the same spot, at the same time you’re trying to protect mother earth.

So far my favorite diving destinations in Asia is,

  1. Komodo National Park, Indonesia. This is without a doubt my favorite place so far. For more information see my previous post
  2. Apo Reef, Philippines. This is right behind number Komodo. Komodo National Park is more diverse and therefore I rate it higher. But I highly doubt that you’ll regret goint to Apo Reef. Check out my earlier post for more information.
