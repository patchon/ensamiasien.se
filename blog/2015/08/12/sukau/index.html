Sukau, Kinabatangand and Osman = Aweseomesauce

Aug 12th, 2015 9:15 am

Almost two weeks ago, before leaving the Perhentian Islands, we had gotten a number to a place called Osman’s Homestay. It was suppose to be quite cheap and apparently this “Osman Guy” knew all the right spots along Kinabatangan.

A day before leaving Sepilok I called Osman’s Homestay and asked them if we could come and stay with them for a few days, the lady agreed and sent us a pickup the next day.

When we arrived at the jetty in Sukau the lady that I’d been talking to over the phone a day earlier greets us with a big smile and directly tells us to jump into the boat. It doesn’t take long before you realise that she probably spent her whole life on this river, knowing both the river and the boat extremely well. I’ve never seen anybody controlling a boat like that. We arrived at their home a couple of minutes later and settled down for a few hours.

We decided to go for an afternoon ride on Kinabatangan, unfortunately I still wasn’t feeling that well so I didn’t really enjoy the ride. We did however see some long tail monkeys, hornbill birds, fishtails and a yellow ringed snake so the ride wasn’t bad, but sitting for hours in the sun when having a fever is really annoying. Osman’s family noticed that something was wrong though and took good care of me (gave me some magic pills that made the fever go away, winning).

Next day we went with Osman on an “elephant search”. The day before he had seen a big herd of elephants a couple of hours away so he knew they couldn’t be too far away (over two hours in a small boat feels pretty long though). Osman has been doing this for over ten years (taking tourists) and is well established among people, he is usually recruited by filming crews because he’s such a good spotter (for example David Attenborough and his film crew used Osman while making the Conquest of the skies documentary). He will take you to the places were nobody else goes and he will definitely point out things that you never would have seen by your self (seriously, he’s that good). After going north on Kinabatangan for almost two and a half hours, we finally caught up with the pygmy elephant herd (counted 40-50). It was truly a magic moment to see these beautiful animals playing around, eating grass and last but not least cooling them selves down in the river. We were totally alone, no other boats, no tourists, nothing - just us and the elephants. Osman simply smiles and tells us to enjoy the wild life.

On the way back we saw a lot of “resort tourist boats” that were packed with people - none of them got to see the elephant herd (simply because the resorts won’t go that extra mile). I strongly recommend the local homestay establishments if you want the best experience.

Before heading over to Kota Kinabalu we decided to go for a night ride on Kinabatangan as well. Mostly we wanted to see the “famous fireflies”, but it turns out that there’s a lot more interesting stuff to see at night than just fireflies. We saw quite a few crocodiles, owls, wild cats, huge rats, jumping fish, bats and lastly sleeping proboscis monkeys. My personal favourite however was the fireflies, it’s just so cool to see them light up as they do. Trees light up in the forest like decorated Christmas trees (seriously, looks like flashing trees). One could just sit there for hours and watch it.
