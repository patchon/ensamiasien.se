Kota Kinabalu, Tampat Do Aman and the Hidden Gem

Aug 18th, 2015 3:03 am

As a last stop before my two friends, Isabel & Louisa, were heading back to Germany we decided to hit the beaches. Borneo is full of nice beaches and Islands, however when it came to actually picking one we seemed to be in trouble.

We only had a few days so our options were somewhat limited, but we finally decided to head over to Kota Kinabalu and then continue north to the ‘tip of Borneo’. There are a couple of places up there but we decided to go with Tampat do aman, a jungle camp run by a guy called Howard Stanton. When we arrived in Kudat a taxi picked us up and took us to the camp where we were introduced to the place by a guy called Gilbert.

There isn’t really much to do in the actual camp so once we got there we directly headed down to the beach. The beach stretches over one kilometer and its white “powderish” sand is simply stunning. The water is crystal clear and the temperature is well above 30 degrees. We had the beach by ourselves for almost three days, and no one of us could understand why (still cant). If you want a getaway and love beaches, this is the place for you.

It’s a real shame that Isabel & Luisa had to go back to Germany, and although saying goodbye was really hard, I do appreciate the time we had together and I’ll always be grateful for you letting me in on your vacation - I seriously couldn’t have asked for a better start on Borneo.

❤


The hidden Gem
There is a small hidden gem near Kudat on the west coast of Borneo called Mañana. This little paradise is truly amazing and you won’t regret going here (call Nina first though to make sure she has available accommodation for you since it can be quite busy).

You will arrive by boat to a beach with some chalets and a small restaurant area, at the end of the beach a diving shop (bigfin divers) is located and operated by the fantastic couple Apec and Ella. The diving here is really good and luckily the coral reefs are still alive since these dive sites basically are untouched (the reason for this is simply that nobody has been operating in this area, you can go diving here both from Kota Kinabalu and Mantanini, but it will cost you and most people just don’t want to pay the price). Be sure to check the weather forecasts though since the weather here can be quite rough and even if you can go diving in rough weather the current can be very very strong which takes away a bit of the fun (personal opinion of course). Also, check out the Bigfin Divers Facebook page for their latest findings.

There is no cellphone reception nor wifi on Mañana which creates a really nice atmosphere in the “hangout area” (just make sure to inform family and friends that you won’t be reachable during the stay). I got to know some great people and I definitely had some good laughs at Mañana - this is a must stop if you are in the area and want some good relaxation combined with meeting some new friends.
