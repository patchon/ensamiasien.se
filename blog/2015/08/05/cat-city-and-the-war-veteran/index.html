Cat City and the War Veteran

Aug 5th, 2015 6:42 pm

This morning we arrived at Kuching, the capital of Sarawak. We had to get up at 4am so we were pretty torn out when we arrived. We fought the tiredness though and decided to get to know the city a little better with a walk. It didn’t take long before we fell in love Kuching and it’s beautiful Sarawak river.

Sarawak River is amazingly cool and runs through the whole city acting as an important transport area. We decided to take one of the tour boats (for 20 ringit) which goes both ways on the river for an hour. The tour boat isn’t really “guided” since it’s only provided by a driver whom didn’t seem to show any interest at all in pointing out stuff for us. Luckily we met a guy called Chris and his wife Charlie who knew the city really well. Turns out that they go to Malaysia twice a year, and that Chris is an old war veteran and was invited by the Malaysian Government this year to celebrate that 50 years had gone by since the conflict between Malaysia and Indonesia ended. Pretty interesting fella who definitely liked to talk (picture with him below).

We ended the day at the Black Bean who happened to serve the best coffee in town. Small, cozy coffee shop with all sorts of coffee and a very friendly staff. If you are up to it, why not enjoy the coffee with some sort of “local fishcake”, it’s suppose to be really good, I however, was not up for it. My friends seemed to like it though.

Lastly, we got totally lost when headed back to Beds Guest House and ended up walking for an hour instead of the “normal 10 minute walk”. Beds Guest House is btw an insanely good guest house with extremely (I mean hotel style) clean rooms and a very friendly staff that will help you with whatever you need. Price is good and we ended up paying 25 ringit each / per night for our own room on the loft. Right outside the guest house you will meet Annie who probably makes one of Kuching’s best Kolo mee, winning!
