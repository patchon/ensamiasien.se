Forget All You Thought You Knew About Diving

Oct 4th, 2015 6:13 am

It has been over two weeks since my last update so I figured a new one would be in place. This time it will be all about diving <3

With my buoyancy pretty much under control and over 40 dives I figured I knew at least the basics about the underwater world (such as turtle’s, white/black tipped sharks, puffer fishes, trigger fishes, nudis, moray eels, etc.). Turns out that I had seen nothing. Literally nothing compared to what you will see if you go to Komodo National Park. The park is huge (total area of 1,733 km²) and the diving is so diverse that you wouldn’t believe it.

Even though this is a very popular diving destination the coral reefs are very healthy (and extremely stunning) and the fishes are just amazing. The visibility is often > 15 meters so you got nothing to complain on there. If there would be something negative about Komodo National Park it basically boils down to two things,

  • The average temperature is between 26 - 28 which usually means that you would want to wear a wetsuit. It’s really nothing weird with that, it’s just that I usually prefer diving without a suit.

  • Some days, at some dive sites, the current can be incredibly strong. Probably the strongest you ever felt. This can be quite fun, since you get to see a lot of “action”, it can also be your worst experience ever. Personally I prefer the calmer dives, even though I appreciate the experience I get from the “crazy, current dives”. Note, there is a big difference between what I call “a crazy, current dive” and a “regular, drift dive”. “Regular drift dives are fine and usually very cool, "crazy, current dives” are, well, crazy.

I stayed with a large group of friends at the newly opened Scuba Junkie Komodo Beach Resort and had a fantastic time. The resort is managed by the dive instructors Rohan and Carys whose professionalism is far beyond anything I’ve come across before. Everything, such as, staff, guidance, equipment, accommodation, food, etc. is extremely professional. Scuba Junkie really got a good thing going here, and I will definitely return - no doubts.

The initial plan was to do four days of diving (three dives per day), but after the second day at a dive site called the couldran I decided, right there and then (seriously, the diving is that good), to extend my stay and do another four days of diving. Totally I ended up with 24 dives in the park and a lot of new friends, both under water and above =)

After Indonesia and Komodo I headed over to the Philippines to meet my friend Gen for some awesome diving and exploring. Since this is my second time in the Philippines I pretty much got my route clear on what I want to do this time, starting off with wreck diving in Coron. Before flying out to Coron we decided to kill a few days in Manila. A city that seems to be misunderstood by the whole world, at least according to Carlos Celdran, who is a performance artist that has very deep knowledge in the history of the Philippines. If you visit Manila, I definitely recommend he’s tour Walk this Way that hopefully will open your eyes regarding the Philippines and Manila (in particular Intramuros).

Very excited about Coron and the wreck diving tomorrow
